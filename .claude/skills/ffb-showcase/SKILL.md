# FFB — The Hype Drop (showcase / sizzle reel)

A single punchy post that gets the league excited about the season's content by **showing a
taste of it**, not describing features. No tech talk, no "how it works" — just real, funny,
personalized samples they can skim in 30 seconds and want more.

## The one rule: show, don't tell
Never write "we'll have power rankings and roasts." **Write the actual power-ranking jab and the
actual roast**, short, so they feel it. Every sample names real owners/teams and is grounded in
`data/` — that's what makes it land instead of reading like a brochure.

## Before you start
- Read `data/lore/storylines.md` (running threads, Crown/Toilet watches), `league-rules.md`
  (cat-5k, Smirnoff Ice, buy-in/stakes), and `data/owners.md` (real names ↔ teams).
- Pull real material from `data/lore/all-time-records.md` (career flexes: Corey's 6 rings, Greg's
  6 podiums / 0 titles, the cellar/cat-5k ledger) and `data/2025/` + `data/lore/2025-season-in-review.md`
  (last season's champ, last place, blowouts, bad beats).
- This is off-season/cold content — **no `ffb-refresh` needed**. It's built on archive + lore.

## Build the reel (aim for ~6 short beats, one line or two each)
Mix so every member sees themselves at least once. Pull from these, pick the sharpest:
1. **Cold open** — a hook that names the stakes (the ring, the cat-5k run in a sports bra, the
   Smirnoff Ice tax). One line.
2. **Power-ranking tease** — the way-too-early #1 with a bullseye + one team's brutal drop line.
3. **Roast sample** — one savage, decision-based line about a real owner's 2025 (a trade, a
   collapse, the title drought).
4. **🍦 Smirnoff Watch** — sell the recurring bit: start a guy who scores ≤0, you owe a video.
   Name last year's offender if there was one.
5. **All-time flex** — one era-proof number from the records (win%, rings, cellar count).
6. **Crown / Toilet Watch** — who's got the target painted on (reigning champ) and who's
   fighting the cat-5k.
Close with a one-line call to action ("this drops all season — matchups, waivers, trades,
recaps. Ball out or get roasted.").

## Output
One chat-ready block, tight and high-energy. Keep it skimmable — headers/emoji are fine, walls
of text are not. Greg reviews before posting to the group + Sleeper chat.

## Tone
Brutal but fun, aimed at decisions/results not people. Hype first — this is the trailer, not the
movie. Leave them wanting the weekly content.
