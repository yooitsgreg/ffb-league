# Five Seven Bro — Story Canon

> The living source of truth for league narratives. Every content piece (matchup breakdowns,
> roasts, power rankings, trade analysis) should pull from this so the canon stays consistent
> instead of being re-derived each time. All entries verified against `data/` — keep it that way.
> Tone reminder: brutal, aimed at decisions/results, not people. Append new threads; don't rewrite history.
> Names: real first names are canon for content; handles in `()` tie back to Sleeper (see `data/owners.md`).
> **Placement caveat (updated 2026-07-01):** for **2011–2024 the archive keeps Top-3 (podium) only** — the full
> 4th-through-last finishing order is intentionally NOT stored (the consolation/toilet bracket carried no stake
> before the 2023 cat-5k, so it's noise). Judge how good a team was by its **regular-season standing**, and treat
> **"finished last" = the regular-season cellar (worst seed)**, not a consolation-bracket placement — the two
> often differ (e.g. 2011 Will finished 12th in consolation but was #9 seed; Evan's 0-14 was the cellar). The
> **cat-5k penalty is separate** and only exists 2023→ (the toilet-bowl loser; see `all-time-records.md`). The
> only playoff facts we can state pre-2025 are the podium (champ, runner-up, 3rd) and that 3rd beat 4th in the
> 3rd-place game — **never assert who beat whom in a semifinal or earlier round.** 2025 is the exception:
> `data/2025/` has the full winners + losers brackets, so those matchups and the cat-5k result are verified.

_Last updated: 2026-07-01 (2011 + 2014 results added from screenshot archives — every season 2011–2025 now documented; 2011 champion Schawn-Paul, 2014 champion Rob; league founded 2011). **2026-07-01b:** last-place reworked into reg-season cellar (all years) vs cat-5k penalty (2023→); corrected stale consolation-last claims — Will 2011, Frank 2013, Josh 2015–16, Ant 2015 seed; pre-2025 archive trimmed to Top-3 placements._

## League-wide running threads
1. **The Chase Trade** — Steve → Corey (Savarese11 → cpearso7), Week 7 2025. The defining transaction; the 1.01 won someone else a ring. Reference point for all future trade content.
2. **Points ≠ trophies** — Ant led the league in scoring and finished 4th; Greg had a losing record and made the final. The league's running argument about luck vs. skill.
3. **The 6-seed that wrecked everyone** — Greg's Cinderella run is the modern playoff benchmark / cautionary tale for higher seeds (looking at you, Will and Ant).
4. **The Toilet King's irony** — Rob named his team The Toilet King and then *avoided* the toilet, dumping Aaron into last (and into the cat 5k). The running gag: the King keeps dodging his own prophecy.
5. **Corey's dynasty** — **six titles (2012, 2015, 2018, 2023–25)**, the winningest manager by a mile (next-most is 2 — Dustin and Rob) and the league's alpha. Won as the #7 seed in 2012 (7-7-0), won as the top seed in 2015, got upset in 2016, won as a 5-8 6th seed in 2018, sat out 2019, returned mediocre for three years, then three-peated. Can win any way he wants. Every 2026 storyline runs through whether the league can finally stop the **four-peat** (with a seventh title on the line).
6. **Aaron's free-fall** — 2024 runner-up → 2025 dead-last + cat 5k. The biggest year-over-year collapse on record; the redemption/relapse question hangs over his 2026.
7. **The seed curse** — regular-season seeding means nothing here, with exactly one exception (below). **Corey won the 2012 championship as a 7-7-0 #7 seed** while the #1 seed (Will's Favre's Disciples, 11-3-0) finished 4th. In 2013: **Preston won as a 7-6 #6 seed** while the #1 seed (chris's Africa, 8-5) finished 3rd. In 2014: **Rob won as a lower seed (8-5)** while Brien's 12-1 juggernaut finished only 3rd and Will's 10-3 finished 4th. In 2016: **Ant won as a 5-8 #6 seed** while the #1 seed (Corey, 10-3) finished 3rd. Then it kept happening: Corey won 2018 as a 5-8 6th seed over Will's 12-1; Dustin won 2019 as a 4-seed; Rob reached that final at 5-8; Will won 2020 as a 4-seed; Dustin won 2021 as a 7-7 5-seed; Greg made the 2025 final as a 6-8 6-seed. Meanwhile the best records flame out consistently: Will 11-3-0 in 2012 → 4th; chris #1 in 2013 → 3rd; Brien 12-1 in 2014 → 3rd; Corey 10-3 in 2016 → 3rd; Will 10-3 in 2017 → 4th; Will 12-1 in 2018 → runner-up (to the 6-seed); Steve 10-3 in 2019 → 4th; Ant 11-2 in 2020 → 3rd; Rob 10-4 in 2022 → out; Steve 11-3 in 2023 → 3rd. **The lone exception: Schawn-Paul won the 2011 founding year as the #1 seed (Buckfutters, 10-4) — the only #1 seed ever to win.** Every #1 seed since has flamed out.
8. **The runner-up graveyard** — a different finals loser almost every year: Corey (2011), Josh (2012), Rob (2013), Tyler (2014), Greg (2015), Preston (2016), Steve (2017), Will (2018), Rob (2019), Retail Tom (2020), Will (2021), Ant (2022), Dustin (2023), Aaron (2024), Greg (2025). Fifteen confirmed seasons (2011–2025); 12 unique runners-up — Rob (2013, 2019), Will (2018, 2021), and Greg (2015, 2025) are the three repeats (Greg lost both his finals to Corey, 10 years apart). Corey's lone runner-up came in 2011, before the dynasty.
9. **From champion to Toilet King** — Rob *won the league twice* — 2014 (on FleaFlicker) and 2017 (10-3, #2 seed) — and nearly won it again in 2019 (runner-up at 5-8), then posted back-to-back elite regular seasons (9-5 in 2021, 10-4 / #1 seed in 2022). Then three straight bottom-three years (6-8, 4-10, 4-10) — the steepest sustained decline in the archive. Two rings exist. He just stopped cashing in.
10. **Three straight worst-to-firsts — then the spell broke.** The basement is literally one draft from a ring here: **Dustin** finished reg-season last in 2018 (3-10) then won 2019; **Will** finished last in 2019 (3-10) then won 2020; **Dustin** finished last again in 2020 (2-11) then won 2021 — three consecutive years the cellar team became champion. Then **Steve** ended the magic: reg-season last **back-to-back in 2021 and 2022** (the only manager to finish last in consecutive years) and turned it into nothing. The cellar is a launchpad for everyone but him.
11. **The heartbreak king (solo)** — Greg is in a class of his own. **Six podiums (2015 2nd, 2017 3rd, 2018 3rd, 2019 3rd, 2022 3rd, 2025 2nd)**, no ring — the most podiums without a title in league history by a wide margin. Lost his first final to Corey in 2015, lost the 2025 final to Corey — the same opponent, 10 years later. Everything in between was just more ways to not win. Ant has 3 post-ring near-misses but has a ring. Greg just doesn't.
12. **Will's 12-1 curse** — The best regular season of the 8-team era (2018, Will's Church of Favre, 12-1 — topped only by Corey's 2024 13-1, tied by Brien's 2014 12-1), and he *lost the championship* to a 5-8 team. The ultimate data point in the seed-curse argument: more than any other season, this proves the regular season is just a formality.
